History Of Treaty 5

Treaty 5 was signed September 20, 1875 between the Federal Government, the Ojibwe peoples, and the Swampy Cree of Lake Winnipeg at Norway House. Treaty 5 includes people from Manitoba, Ontario, and Saskatchewan. For those who signed, they would gain $5 on initial sign up which is far less when compared to treaty 3 and 4 which were given $12 on signing. Also, a family unit was given 160 acres of land. Annually $5 was given per regular person, $15 was given per headman, $25 was given per chief. In the 1900's a man named William Mclean was a part of the Department of Indian Affairs. He said that Indigenous people did not fully understand the contracts and what they entailed. Today the canadian government acknowledges the land claims and they allow for indigenous and aboriginal people live on the land they are entitled to. The reserves that indigenous people live on today are not the best as they are far from hospitals and resources.
