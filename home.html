History

Treaty 5 was signed September 20, 1875 between the Federal Government, the Ojibwe peoples, and the Swampy Cree of Lake Winnipeg at Norway House. Treaty 5 includes people from Manitoba, Ontario, and Saskatchewan. For those who signed, they would gain $5 on initial sign up which is far less when compared to treaty 3 and 4 which were given $12 on signing. Also, a family unit was given 160 acres of land. Annually $5 was given per regular person, $15 was given per headman, $25 was given per chief. In the 1900's a man named William Mclean was a part of the Department of Indian Affairs. He said that Indigenous people did not fully understand the contracts and what they entailed.

Sponsour

NAN or Nishnawbe Aski Nation is our main sponsour at Treaty 5. NAN represents 49 indigenous groups related to treaty 9 and treaty 5. NAN strives for local self-determination while establishing spiritual, cultural, social, and economic independence.
